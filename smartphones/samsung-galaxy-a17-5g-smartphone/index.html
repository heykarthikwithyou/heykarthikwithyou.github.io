Short Description

An earlier iteration of the Galaxy was the very first phone we tried with our now-teen, before the current crop of kid-specific models hit the market. If you’re looking for a first phone that balances flexibility, safety tools, and price, the Samsung Galaxy A17 is a strong pick.

There’s one big reason for this. I’m an Apple girl myself, but Android phones like this tend to work better with parental-control apps such as Bark and Aura. This means the software can monitor messages and activity more thoroughly than they can on iPhones. The A17 also feels surprisingly premium for a budget phone, thanks to its large 6.7-inch AMOLED display and solid everyday performance.

That said, the base model only includes 4GB of RAM, so heavy multitaskers may notice some lag. It wasn’t an issue for our kid, but it depends on the ways each kid uses their phone. The big screen does make this phone a little bulky for small hands—but for most tweens, that trade-off is worth it.

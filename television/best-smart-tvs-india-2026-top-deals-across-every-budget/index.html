Buying a smart TV in 2026 is less about choosing the biggest screen and more about matching the right TV to your room size, viewing habits, and budget. QLED and Mini LED TVs have become more affordable, gaming features are appearing in mainstream models, and smart TV platforms like Google TV and webOS make streaming easier than ever.

For this guide, we selected eight popular 65-inch and 75-inch models across value, gaming, Mini LED, and premium brand categories. The goal is simple: help you identify which TV should you buy in 2026 based on room size, budget, and usage, without getting lost in marketing jargon.

Quick Highlights

  1. Best Budget 65-inch QLED: VW 65-inch Pro Series QLED Google TV
  2. Best Gaming TV: Hisense 65E7Q PRO (144Hz Game Mode Pro)
  3. Best 75-inch Value TV: Xiaomi 75-inch X Pro Series QLED Google TV
  4. Best Picture Quality: Philips 65-inch QD-Mini LED Google TV
  5. Best Value Mini LED TV: TCL 65Q6C QD-Mini LED Google TV

Analysis

For pure value, the VW 65-inch Pro Series QLED is hard to ignore. It gives buyers a large 65-inch QLED screen with Google TV and 4K support at a price that is usually far lower than mainstream premium brands. Picture quality is not Mini LED-class, but for movies, streaming, and everyday family viewing it delivers a surprisingly strong big-screen experience.

Gamers should look first at the Hisense 65E7Q PRO. The standout feature is the 144Hz Game Mode Pro, which is uncommon in this price segment. Combined with Dolby Vision, Dolby Atmos, and AI upscaling, it offers one of the strongest gaming-and-entertainment packages in the list. The trade-off is that VIDAA's app ecosystem is smaller than Google TV's.

If your priority is a cinematic living-room setup, the Xiaomi 75-inch X Pro Series QLED gives you a massive 75-inch screen with Google TV and QLED color reproduction. It is one of the most accessible ways to get a true home-theater-sized display without moving into flagship TV pricing. Room size matters here; this TV works best in larger spaces with a viewing distance of roughly 8–12 feet or more.

For buyers who care most about picture quality, the Philips 65-inch QD-Mini LED is a standout. Mini LED backlighting provides much better contrast and HDR impact than standard LED or basic QLED TVs. Dark scenes look deeper, highlights pop more, and overall image quality feels more premium. It is a stronger movie-and-streaming TV than most conventional QLED models in the same size.

The TCL 65Q6C QD-Mini LED is arguably the best value Mini LED option in the lineup. It delivers many of the contrast and brightness advantages of Mini LED technology while staying more aggressively priced than flagship competitors. For buyers comparing standard QLED TVs against Mini LED TVs, this is often the tipping point model that makes Mini LED worth the upgrade.

Brand-focused buyers will gravitate toward Sony BRAVIA 2M2 65-inch, Samsung 75-inch Crystal UHD Vision AI, and LG 65-inch NU87 AI Series. Sony wins on picture processing and natural color reproduction, Samsung on large-screen smart features and AI enhancements, and LG on its polished webOS experience and family-friendly usability. These models may not dominate every specification chart, but they score highly on long-term ownership confidence, service network strength, and everyday ease of use.

How to Choose the Right Smart TV in 2026

Start with room size and viewing distance. For most Indian living rooms, 65 inches is the sweet spot. Choose 75 inches only if you have enough space and typically sit farther away. A large TV in a small room can be overwhelming and may expose lower-quality content.

Next, choose the display tier. Standard LED TVs are the cheapest, QLED TVs improve color and brightness, and Mini LED TVs deliver the biggest jump in contrast, HDR performance, and black levels. If movies and streaming are your main use case, Mini LED is usually worth the extra money.

Finally, match the TV to your usage. Gamers should prioritize refresh rate and low latency, movie lovers should prioritize Mini LED and HDR quality, and family buyers should prioritize a stable smart platform, service network, and ease of use. Google TV offers the broadest app ecosystem, while webOS and Samsung's platform are known for smooth everyday operation.

FAQs

Which is better in 2026: QLED or Mini LED?

Mini LED is generally better because it offers higher brightness, deeper blacks, and stronger HDR performance. QLED mainly improves color over standard LED TVs.

Is 75 inches too big for an Indian living room?

Not if you sit roughly 8–12 feet or more from the screen. For smaller rooms, 65 inches is usually the safer choice.

What is the best gaming TV in this list?

The Hisense 65E7Q PRO because of its 144Hz Game Mode Pro and gaming-focused features.

Do I need a soundbar?

For casual viewing, built-in speakers are fine. For movies, sports, and large rooms, a soundbar is highly recommended.

Is Google TV better than VIDAA or webOS?

Google TV has the broadest app ecosystem. webOS is very smooth and user-friendly, while VIDAA is simpler and generally has fewer apps.

Final Thoughts

The best smart TV in 2026 is not simply the one with the highest specification sheet—it's the one that matches your room, budget, and viewing habits. Budget buyers should focus on value QLED models, gamers should prioritize refresh rate and latency, and movie enthusiasts should spend the extra money on Mini LED. If you choose a TV based on how you actually watch content rather than chasing every feature, you'll get a much better long-term upgrade.

How to Choose the Right Projector

When buying a projector, brightness is the most important factor. Look for ANSI or ISO lumens instead of exaggerated lumen claims. For well-lit rooms, choose at least 800–1200 ANSI lumens, while lower brightness models are suitable for dark environments.

Next, consider smart features and connectivity. Projectors with Google TV or Android OS provide a better experience with built-in apps. Features like WiFi 6, Bluetooth, and HDMI ARC improve usability and connectivity with other devices.

Finally, think about your usage. If you need portability, go for compact models. For home theater setups, prioritize brightness and screen size. Always balance features with real-world performance to get the best value.
